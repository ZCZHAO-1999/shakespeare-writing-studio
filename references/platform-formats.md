# Platform Formats

Shackspare is not limited to Medium or Substack. Choose the format before editing.

## 1. Medium Essay

Best for:

- public ideas
- personal essays with a useful lesson
- product or technology arguments
- career and learning essays

Layout:

- strong title
- subtitle/deck
- short opening
- clear section headings
- occasional pull quote
- minimal charts unless the essay is analytical

Voice:

- personal but not diaristic
- clear and accessible
- one argument carried to a landing

## 2. Substack Post

Best for:

- recurring audience relationship
- field notes
- opinionated analysis
- reflective updates

Layout:

- more direct opening
- optional personal note
- fewer polished transitions
- stronger sense of the writer speaking to known readers

Voice:

- warmer
- more conversational
- more tolerant of open questions

## 3. Founder / Operator Note

Best for:

- company updates
- product thinking
- lessons learned
- internal-to-public essays

Layout:

- operational pain
- decision made
- tradeoff
- result
- what remains unresolved

Voice:

- plain
- accountable
- concrete
- no victory-lap language

## 4. Executive Memo

Best for:

- internal strategy
- investment thinking
- decision support
- board or leadership communication

Layout:

- executive summary
- recommendation
- evidence
- risks
- open questions
- next actions

Voice:

- compressed
- direct
- source-aware
- low ornament

## 5. Op-Ed

Best for:

- public argument
- policy or industry stance
- contrarian viewpoint

Layout:

- sharp thesis
- stakes
- evidence
- counterargument
- call or conclusion

Voice:

- confident
- fair to objections
- memorable without being theatrical

## 6. Speech / Keynote

Best for:

- live delivery
- founder talks
- graduation / event remarks
- product launches

Layout:

- spoken opening
- story or scene
- repeated anchor phrase
- 3–5 beats
- final image

Voice:

- oral
- rhythmic
- simpler sentences
- no dense citations in the main body

## 7. Research-Backed Article

Best for:

- market analysis
- trend analysis
- industry explainers
- data-led arguments

Layout:

- question
- source base
- evidence sequence
- charts or data cards
- interpretation
- limits

Voice:

- measured
- explicit about uncertainty
- clear about source quality
