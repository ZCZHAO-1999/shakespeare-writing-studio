# Human Voice / Anti-AI Editing

The goal is not to "beat detectors." The goal is to make the piece more truthful, specific, and human.

## 1. AI-Sounding Patterns To Remove

Delete or rewrite:

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It is important to note..."
- "This highlights the importance of..."
- "Not only X, but also Y..."
- "By leveraging..."
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "A testament to..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Delve into..."
- "Robust, seamless, transformative, cutting-edge" when unsupported
- repeated three-part lists in every paragraph
- symmetrical paragraphs with the same rhythm
- conclusions that restate the article

## 2. Human Voice Moves

Use:

- one arguable point of view before structure
- short plain sentences when the idea is sharp
- longer sentences when the writer is thinking through a tradeoff
- one specific noun instead of three abstract nouns
- lived constraints
- small uncertainty where the writer is genuinely unsure
- concrete verbs
- sensory or operational detail
- one surprising but accurate comparison

## 3. Point Of View Test

Before rewriting, write the essay's stance in one sentence.

Good stances:

- make a directional claim
- name who wins, loses, pays, or changes behavior
- admit the main uncertainty
- can be argued against by a smart reader

Weak stances:

- "This trend is important."
- "There are opportunities and challenges."
- "The future remains uncertain."
- "This article explores..."

If the stance is weak, do not polish the prose yet. Rebuild the argument first.

## 4. Specificity Ladder

Weak:

```text
Teams need better communication to drive alignment.
```

Better:

```text
The handoff broke in the Slack thread, not in the strategy doc.
```

Weak:

```text
AI is transforming how people work.
```

Better:

```text
The first useful AI tools did not replace the meeting. They made the meeting shorter.
```

## 5. Read-Aloud Test

Read the piece aloud.

Mark any sentence where:

- the mouth gets bored
- the rhythm becomes too even
- the writer disappears
- a phrase sounds like a brochure
- a paragraph says something technically true but emotionally dead

Rewrite those lines first.

## 6. Detector-Aware, Not Detector-Obsessed

AI detectors are unreliable. Do not optimize for a score at the cost of truth.

Still, writing tends to feel less AI-like when it has:

- verifiable specifics
- personal stakes
- uneven but intentional rhythm
- concrete scenes
- domain-specific constraints
- real uncertainty
- non-generic transitions

## 7. Final Human Pass

Before delivery:

- remove one unnecessary abstraction per section
- replace weak verbs with concrete verbs
- cut any paragraph that only introduces the next paragraph
- vary paragraph length
- add one grounded example where the argument floats
- make sure the ending could only belong to this essay
