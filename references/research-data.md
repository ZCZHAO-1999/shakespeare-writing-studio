# Research And Data Skill

Use this module when the article needs external evidence, public statistics, market data, policy context, economic indicators, or factual verification.

Do not use it just to decorate an essay.

## 1. When To Search

Search when:

- the user asks for latest/current/recent information
- the article makes factual claims about markets, companies, policy, demographics, economics, science, or technology
- numbers would materially change the argument
- a chart is being considered
- source credibility matters

Do not search when:

- the piece is primarily personal reflection
- the point is conceptual and does not depend on current facts
- the user explicitly says not to browse

## 2. Source Priority

Prefer:

- official statistics agencies
- company filings and annual reports
- reputable research institutions
- academic papers
- platform/company documentation
- high-quality journalism with named sources

Use with caution:

- SEO content
- unsourced market-size blog posts
- anonymous charts
- screenshots without original links
- AI-generated summaries

## 3. Research Output

Before writing, create a source table:

| Claim | Source | Date | Number / Fact | Confidence | Use In Article? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- Keep source URLs.
- Keep dates.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Do not over-precision vague numbers.
- Mark missing data as `[TK]`.

## 4. Research Depth

Light research:

- 3–5 sources
- used for fact-checking or context

Standard research:

- 6–10 sources
- used for evidence-backed essays

Deep research:

- 10+ sources
- used only when the piece is explicitly research-led

## 5. Writing With Data

Good data writing does not dump numbers.

Use this order:

1. question
2. number
3. source/date
4. plain-English interpretation
5. limitation or caveat
6. implication

Bad:

```text
The market is growing rapidly, with various reports showing significant expansion.
```

Better:

```text
[Source] estimated [number] in [year]. The useful point is not the exact figure; it is that demand moved from experimental budget to operating budget.
```

## 6. Citation Style

For Medium/Substack-style pieces:

- link naturally in the paragraph
- avoid academic footnote clutter unless needed
- include a source appendix for research-heavy articles

For memos:

- use source table or footnotes
- keep citations close to claims
