# Editorial Lessons / 实战修正规则

Use this file as the memory of hard-won editorial corrections. These rules apply to Chinese and English outputs.

## 1. The Root Is The Article, Not The README

- A polished README cannot rescue weak content.
- The article needs a stance, a title with tension, and a structure that advances the argument.
- Do not flatten controversial topics into neutral summaries.
- If the article has no point of view, stop polishing and rebuild the spine.

## 2. Titles Must Carry Search And Desire

- Use the user's intended title direction when it is sharper than the draft.
- A strong title should contain the actor, event, consequence, and unresolved question.
- Keep search terms visible when discovery matters: company names, product names, platform names, and category words.
- Avoid vague titles such as "a new era," "rethinking X," or "what it means."
- Do not replace a user-provided sharp title with a safer analytical headline.

Example:

```text
Fable 5 被封禁，智谱冲万亿。中国模型登顶日即将来临？
```

## 3. No Invented Strategic Frames

Do not invent terms that the article does not support.

Reject:
- backup model
- dual-vendor era
- model procurement entering a new age
- route layer
- supply router
- generic "second supplier" logic

If the source material supports only a market narrative, write the market narrative. If it supports product architecture, then write architecture.

## 4. Anti-AI Prose Rules

Remove:
- "不是 A, 而是 B" scaffolding
- "not only X, but also Y" scaffolding
- "立场:" labels inside covers or pull quotes
- generic three-part symmetry in every section
- phrases that sound like a model proving it can reason

Prefer:
- direct judgment
- concrete actor behavior
- verifiable facts
- uneven but intentional sentence rhythm
- paragraphs that sound written, not assembled

## 5. WeChat Reading Rhythm

WeChat quality is mostly typography, spacing, image placement, and reading breath.

Rules:
- First screen must show cover, headline, deck, and a clear conflict.
- Body font must be large enough for phone reading.
- Paragraph rhythm should vary; no long gray wall of text.
- Images need setup and follow-up sentences.
- The reader should glide from headline to deck to first paragraph without friction.

## 6. Cover Images Must Not Lower The Article

A cover must win the first glance. It should not force the reader to decode an abstract system.

Reject immediately:
- literal mountain / flag / summit graphics unless the article is truly about a mountain or expedition
- cheap arrows that pretend to show momentum
- generic node maps
- abstract route diagrams that only the author understands
- decorative "premium dark" plates used across unrelated topics
- tiny labels that vanish in mobile preview
- "stance:" boxes or other essay-outline labels

Prefer:
- strong title zone
- one visual conflict
- 2-3 article-specific signals
- editorial information board
- redaction / stamp / filing / market tape when the story is regulatory or financial
- restrained color and generous negative space

For a finance / tech event cover, a clean information board often beats a metaphor.

## 7. Visual Style Must Follow The Story

- Do not default to dark background.
- Do not default to light cards either.
- Choose the visual world from the article's industry, mood, and evidence.
- A regulatory shock can use stamps, filings, notices, timelines, or market boards.
- A data-heavy research piece can use statistical atlases, heatmaps, slope charts, network clouds, or annotated evidence maps.
- A product story can use workflow surfaces and interaction traces.

## 8. Creative Data Graphics

Statistical visuals can be creative, but only when the data supports it.

Use:
- annotated slope charts
- small-multiple panels
- heatmaps
- bubble networks
- calendar / timeline distributions
- evidence maps
- word clouds only when text frequency is the subject

Avoid:
- charts added to look serious
- unsupported numbers
- overpacked dashboards
- legends too small for mobile
- beautiful but unreadable graphics

## 9. Search Quality

When background research is needed, use top-tier sources only.

Allowed:
- official company posts and docs
- regulators and public agencies
- filings, court records, standards bodies
- research institutions and primary datasets
- reputable named reporting

Rejected:
- SEO summaries
- repost farms
- unsourced stats pages
- screenshots without source trail
- AI-summary pages

## 10. Unified Skill Scope

This is one writing studio:
- Chinese WeChat
- English Medium / Substack
- research notes
- op-eds
- speeches
- data storytelling
- charts and information graphics

Do not split Chinese and English into separate skill systems. Select modules by platform and job.
