# Medium / Substack Writing Patterns

This guide distills repeatable patterns for strong English internet essays.

## 1. What Good Pieces Have In Common

Strong Medium/Substack essays often share these traits:

- a concrete opening
- a clear reader promise
- personal or professional stakes
- examples before abstraction
- sections that move the argument forward
- one strong idea, not six half-ideas
- an ending that leaves a residue

## 2. Opening Patterns

### A. Moment Opening

Start with a specific moment.

Example:

```text
The first time I noticed the problem, it was buried in a calendar invite.
```

Use when:

- the writer has a personal story
- the topic could otherwise sound abstract
- the essay needs warmth quickly

### B. Tension Opening

Start with a contradiction the reader recognizes.

Example:

```text
Everyone says they want better tools. Most teams actually want fewer decisions.
```

Use when:

- the piece is analytical
- the argument challenges a common belief
- the reader needs a reason to keep going

### C. Observation Opening

Start with a precise observation.

Example:

```text
The most revealing part of a product demo is usually the moment after the demo ends.
```

Use when:

- the writer has domain expertise
- the essay is about work, craft, or technology

### D. Confession Opening

Start with a small admission.

Example:

```text
I used to think consistency meant repeating the same process. I was wrong.
```

Use carefully. It works only if the rest of the essay earns the admission.

## 3. Article Types

### Personal Essay With An Idea

Structure:

1. specific moment
2. what the writer believed then
3. what changed
4. what the reader can see differently
5. landing image or thought

### Analytical Essay

Structure:

1. tension
2. old model
3. what changed
4. example
5. consequences
6. boundary conditions

### Founder / Operator Note

Structure:

1. operational pain
2. failed default solution
3. decision made
4. tradeoff
5. result or open question
6. lesson without grandstanding

### Product / Technology Essay

Structure:

1. user moment
2. old workflow
3. mechanism
4. why it matters
5. what still breaks
6. future direction

## 4. Section Logic

Each section should do one job:

- tell a scene
- explain a mechanism
- challenge a belief
- introduce evidence
- handle an objection
- clarify the stakes
- land the argument

Avoid section titles like:

- Introduction
- Background
- The Problem
- The Solution
- Conclusion

Prefer section titles that carry thought:

- The cost was hiding in the handoff
- The tool worked until the team grew
- Speed changed the wrong part of the system
- A better answer still needs a next step

## 5. Ending Patterns

### Return To Image

Return to the opening scene with a changed meaning.

### Narrow Landing

End on the smallest useful truth, not the largest possible claim.

### Open Door

End with a real unresolved question.

Avoid:

- "In conclusion"
- "Only time will tell"
- "The future is bright"
- "This is just the beginning"
